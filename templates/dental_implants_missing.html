INNOVATIVE TECHNIQUE

The Socket Shield Technique

Revolutionary Approach to Implant Placement

What is Socket Shield?

The socket shield technique involves leaving a thin portion of the tooth root (buccal plate) in place when extracting the tooth, serving as a biological barrier to prevent bone resorption.

Preserves Natural Architecture

Maintains the natural emergence profile and soft tissue contours around the implant, creating seamless aesthetics.

Reduces Bone Loss

Significantly decreases post-extraction bone resorption, preserving the natural ridge dimensions.

Pocket Friendly

Completely avoids bone grafting and membrane placement thus becoming pocket friendly for the patient.

PRECISION SURGERY

Guided Implant Surgery

A highly accurate, computer-guided procedure that uses 3D CT/CBCT scans to plan and place dental implants with greater precision than traditional freehand methods.

Technology & Planning

Uses 3D technology (CBCT) and software to visualize anatomical structures (nerves, sinuses) and plan the ideal implant position.

The Surgical Guide

A customized, computer-generated template created with sleeves that guide drills and the implant to the exact, pre-planned position.

Procedure Types

Static: Physical guide for drilling | Dynamic: Navigation software for real-time positioning (similar to GPS)

Benefits
  • Precision: Achieves ideal, anatomically safe implant placement
  • Minimally Invasive: Often allows "flapless" surgery with less pain and faster healing
  • Efficiency: Reduced chair time and higher patient comfort
  • Clinical Success: Survival rates of 89% to 100%

This approach is particularly valuable for complex cases, full-mouth reconstructions, or where precise, aesthetic results are critical.

SOCKET SHIELD

The Socket Shield Technique

One of the methods of replacing a single tooth is a procedure called Socket Shield Technique.

It's an innovative procedure that preserves a portion of your natural tooth root, maintaining the surrounding bone and gum tissue for optimal aesthetics and long-term stability. Completely avoids bone grafting and membrane placement thus becoming pocket friendly for the patient.

THE PROCESS

The Implant Procedure

Comprehensive Evaluation

3D CBCT imaging and digital planning to determine optimal implant position.

Socket Shield Preparation - if required / Previous teeth present

Meticulous preparation of the remaining root fragment to preserve surrounding tissues.

Implant Placement

Precise surgical placement of the titanium implant in the prepared socket.

Final Restoration

Custom crown fabrication and placement after osseointegration (3-4 months).

RECOVERY

Healing and Recovery

Week 1-2

Initial healing with minimal discomfort. Soft tissues begin to close around the implant site.

Month 1-3

Osseointegration begins as the implant fuses with the jawbone. Temporary restoration may be placed.

Month 3-4

Complete bone integration. Final crown placement and full functionality restored.
