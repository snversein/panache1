Comprehensive surgical solutions for chronic TMJ disorders and joint replacement

When is TMJ Surgery Recommended?

  • Severe Arthritis: Advanced degenerative joint disease unresponsive to conservative treatment
  • Traumatic Injury: Fractured or severely damaged temporomandibular joint
  • Failed Previous Surgery: Complications from previous TMJ procedures
  • Progressive Dysfunction: Worsening jaw function despite non-surgical treatment
  • Chronic Pain: Severe, persistent pain affecting quality of life

Our Surgical Options:

Arthroscopy

Minimally invasive procedure using small cameras and instruments

Joint Replacement (TMJR)

Total joint replacement with custom prosthetic devices

Discectomy

Removal of damaged disc tissue to relieve pain and improve function

Joint Reconstruction

Complex reconstruction using bone grafts and specialized techniques

Surgical Process:

Pre-Operative Planning

3D imaging and custom prosthesis design

Surgery

2-4 hour procedure under general anesthesia

Hospital Stay

1-3 days depending on procedure complexity

Rehabilitation

Physical therapy and gradual jaw exercises

TMJ Disorder Symptoms

  • Jaw pain or tenderness
  • Difficulty chewing
  • Clicking or popping sounds
  • Locking of jaw joint
  • Headaches and ear pain
  • Difficulty opening mouth wide
Recovery Timeline
  • Week 1-2: Soft diet, limited jaw movement
  • Week 2-4: Gradual increase in jaw activity
  • Week 4-8: Physical therapy begins
  • Month 3-6: Return to normal function
