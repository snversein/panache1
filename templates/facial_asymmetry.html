SCIENCE OF FACIAL BALANCE

Advanced techniques for achieving facial harmony and symmetry

Facial asymmetry refers to imbalanced proportions and alignment of facial features. When one side of the face appears different from the other, it can affect overall appearance and self-confidence. Our advanced surgical techniques address both bony structures and soft tissues to create a harmonious, natural look.

Using digital analysis, precise measurements, and surgical planning, we can correct asymmetries resulting from trauma, developmental issues, or natural aging. The goal is facial harmony that appears natural and balanced.

TYPES OF ASYMMETRY

Common Types of Facial Asymmetry

Congenital Asymmetry

Present from birth, affecting natural facial development and creating noticeable imbalance between facial sides.

Traumatic Asymmetry

Resulting from accidents or injuries affecting facial structure, causing uneven appearance.

Developmental Issues

Growth abnormalities during development affecting facial proportions and symmetry.

Age-Related Changes

Natural aging causing gradual volume loss and facial asymmetry over time.
