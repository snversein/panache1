What is Bone Regeneration?

In modern dentistry, Advanced Bone Regeneration refers to sophisticated techniques and materials used to rebuild jawbone that has been lost due to tooth extraction, gum disease, or trauma.

Think of it as "rebuilding the foundation." Just as you wouldn't build a house on shifting sand, you can't place a dental implant or a stable bridge in a jawbone that is too thin or weak.

Our advanced bone regeneration procedures help create a strong, stable foundation for:

  • Dental Implants - Providing secure anchorage for replacement teeth
  • Dental Bridges - Ensuring stable support for prosthetic teeth
  • Better Oral Health - Preventing further bone loss and maintaining facial structure
  • Natural Appearance - Preserving facial contours and smile aesthetics

Advanced Bone Regeneration Solutions

Building the Foundation for Dental Success

Guided Bone Regeneration (GBR) is a sophisticated surgical procedure that rebuilds lost bone structure in the jaw, creating the ideal foundation for dental implants or preserving facial aesthetics. When bone loss occurs due to periodontal disease, trauma, or tooth extraction, GBR techniques can regenerate natural bone.

Our bone regeneration approach combines advanced biomaterials with specialized membranes to guide new bone growth in specific areas. This predictable procedure restores both height and width of jawbone, enabling successful implant placement even in challenging cases.

TECHNIQUES

Sinus Lift: Direct vs Indirect

Understanding the two approaches to sinus augmentation

Indirect Sinus Lift (Osteotome Technique)

When used: Suitable for cases requiring 1-4mm of bone height increase.

Procedure: The sinus floor is gently lifted through the implant site using specialized osteotome instruments, without creating a surgical window.

Advantages:

  • Less invasive with minimal trauma
  • Faster healing time
  • Can be done simultaneously with implant placement
  • Reduced postoperative discomfort

Direct Sinus Lift (Lateral Window Technique)

When used: Required for cases needing more than 4-5mm of bone height increase.

Procedure: A surgical window is created in the sinus wall to access the sinus cavity. The sinus membrane is carefully lifted, and bone graft material is placed beneath it.

Advantages:

  • Allows for greater bone augmentation
  • Better visualization of sinus anatomy
  • More predictable results for large defects
  • Can correct severe bone loss cases
Which Technique is Right for You?

Our expert team will evaluate your specific case through 3D CT scanning to determine the most appropriate sinus lift technique for your unique situation and implant needs.

PROCEDURE

Ridge Split Procedure

An innovative technique for horizontal bone augmentation

What is Ridge Split?

Ridge split (also called ridge expansion or bone splitting) is a surgical technique used to increase the width of a narrow jawbone, allowing for dental implant placement without the need for extensive bone grafting.

How It Works:

  1. Precise Division: The ridge is carefully split lengthwise using specialized instruments
  2. Gentle Expansion: The split edges are gradually widened to create space
  3. Bone Grafting: Bone graft material is placed in the created space
  4. Implant Placement: Dental implants can often be placed simultaneously

Benefits:

  • Minimally invasive alternative to block grafting
  • Reduces treatment time significantly
  • Often allows immediate implant placement
  • Preserves existing bone structure
  • Faster healing and recovery
Ideal for Narrow Ridges

Perfect for cases where the jawbone is too narrow for implants but has adequate height

Time Saving

Significantly reduces overall treatment time compared to traditional bone grafting

Proven technique with excellent long-term outcomes when performed by experienced surgeons

MATERIALS

GBR Components & Materials

Bone Graft Materials

Various options including autograft (patient's own bone), allograft (donor bone), alloplasts (synthetic), and xenografts (animal-derived) for different clinical situations.

Barrier Membranes

Resorbable and non-resorbable membranes that prevent soft tissue invasion, allowing bone cells to regenerate undisturbed in the treatment area.

Growth Factors

Biologically active proteins (PRF, BMP) that stimulate and accelerate bone formation for enhanced healing and predictable results.

Fixation Devices

Titanium screws and tacks that secure membranes in place, ensuring stable conditions for optimal bone regeneration.

THE PROCESS

The GBR Procedure

Comprehensive Evaluation

3D imaging and bone density assessment to determine exact bone requirements and plan GBR procedure.

Site Preparation

Careful removal of any compromised tissue and creation of space for bone graft material placement.

Graft Placement

Precise placement of bone graft material in the deficient area, ensuring proper contour and volume.

Membrane Application

Placement of barrier membrane to protect graft and direct bone growth in desired areas.

Suturing & Healing

Secure closure with sutures and protective dressing for optimal healing conditions.

RECOVERY

Recovery and Healing Process

Week 1-2

Initial healing with mild swelling and discomfort. Soft diet and gentle oral hygiene to protect the surgical site.

Week 3-6

Early bone formation begins. Most discomfort resolves. Gradual return to normal activities and diet.

Month 3-6

Significant bone maturation. Follow-up imaging to assess bone regeneration progress and plan next steps.

Month 6-9

Complete bone integration achieved. Site ready for implant placement or final restoration.
