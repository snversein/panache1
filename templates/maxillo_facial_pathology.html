Maxillo-Facial Pathology

Specialized treatment for cysts and tumors in the jaws

Jaw Pathology Management

Expert diagnosis and treatment of cysts, tumors, and other pathologies affecting the maxillofacial region

Conditions We Treat:

Odontogenic Cysts

Radicular cysts, dentigerous cysts, and other cysts arising from tooth development

Non-Odontogenic Cysts

Nasopalatine duct cysts, nasolabial cysts, and other developmental cysts

Benign Tumors

Ameloblastoma, odontogenic keratocyst, and other non-cancerous growths

Malignant Tumors

Early detection and comprehensive treatment of jaw cancers

Our Diagnostic Approach:

Advanced Imaging

Digital X-rays, CT scans, and MRI for precise diagnosis

Histopathology

Biopsy and laboratory analysis for accurate diagnosis

Expert Evaluation

Specialized assessment by experienced maxillofacial surgeons

Treatment Options:

  • Surgical Excision: Complete removal of cysts and tumors with preservation of surrounding structures
  • Reconstruction: Restoration of form and function using bone grafts and advanced techniques
  • Reconstruction: Regular monitoring for early detection of any changes
  • Adjuvant Therapy: Coordinated care with oncology when needed

Warning Signs

Seek immediate evaluation if you notice:

  • Swelling that doesn't resolve
  • Painless lumps or growths
  • Numbness in face or mouth
  • Loose teeth without cause
  • Difficulty chewing or swallowing
  • Unexplained bleeding
  • Changes in bite or speech
Early Detection Saves Lives

Regular dental check-ups can help detect pathological conditions early when treatment is most effective.

Most jaw pathologies are discovered during routine dental examinations
