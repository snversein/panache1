ADVANCED TREATMENT

Modern Root Canal Therapy

Advanced Treatment for Tooth Preservation

Root canal treatment (RCT) is a highly effective procedure designed to save teeth that would otherwise need extraction. When the dental pulp becomes infected or damaged, our advanced endodontic techniques remove the infected tissue while preserving the natural tooth structure.

Using high magnification dentalloupes and biocompatible materials, we ensure thorough cleaning and precise filling of the root canal system, allowing you to keep your natural tooth and avoid more extensive restorative work.

INDICATIONS

When is Root Canal Treatment Needed?

Severe Tooth Pain

Intense, persistent pain especially when chewing or applying pressure to the tooth.

Dental Abscess

Pus-filled pockets at the tooth root causing swelling, pain, and potential systemic infection.

Deep Decay

Cavities that have reached the tooth pulp, causing infection and inflammation of the nerve tissue.

Chipped/Broken Tooth

Trauma exposing the pulp chamber to bacteria, requiring immediate treatment to prevent infection.

THE PROCESS

The Root Canal Procedure

Diagnostic Assessment

X-ray imaging and pulp vitality testing to confirm diagnosis and plan treatment approach.

Anesthesia & Isolation

Local anesthesia for complete comfort. Rubber dam placement to keep the tooth dry and isolated.

Pulp Chamber Access

Small opening created in the tooth crown to access the infected pulp tissue and root canals.

Canal Cleaning & Shaping

Removal of infected pulp and thorough cleaning of all root canals using microscopic techniques.

Canal Filling

Sealing of cleaned canals with biocompatible gutta-percha material to prevent reinfection.

Final Restoration

Placement of temporary or permanent crown to protect and restore tooth function.

AFTERCARE

Recovery and Aftercare

Day 1-3

Initial healing with mild discomfort. Avoid chewing on the treated tooth and follow prescribed medications.

Week 1

Most discomfort resolves. Tooth becomes less sensitive. Return to normal activities as comfort allows.

Week 2-3

Complete healing of surrounding tissues. Final crown placement if needed. Normal chewing function restored.

Long-Term Care

Regular dental check-ups and good oral hygiene ensure long-term success of the treated tooth.
