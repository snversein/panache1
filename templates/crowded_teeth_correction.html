What Are Crowded Teeth?

Crowded teeth occur when there isn't enough space in the jaw for all teeth to align properly. This leads to overlapping, twisting, or displacement of teeth, affecting both appearance and oral health. Crowding can range from mild to severe and may worsen over time if left untreated.

Why Should Crowded Teeth Be Corrected?

While crowded teeth can impact your smile, the concerns go beyond aesthetics:

  • Difficult cleaning → Increased risk of plaque buildup, cavities, and gum disease
  • Bite problems → Improper chewing and jaw discomfort
  • Speech issues → In some cases, alignment affects pronunciation
  • Tooth wear → Uneven pressure can lead to enamel damage

Correcting crowding improves both function and long-term oral health.

How Do Braces Fix Crowded Teeth?

Braces work by applying continuous, gentle pressure to gradually move teeth into proper alignment.

The Process:

1
Initial Consultation

A detailed examination, X-rays, and digital scans with our iTero Lumina scanner help determine the severity of crowding.

2
Simulation

In most cases we can show you your final result of teeth movement with the help of our iTero Lumina Scanner.

3
Treatment Planning

Your orthodontist then designs a personalized plan. In some cases, space creation methods like tooth reshaping or extractions may be needed.

4
Placement of Braces

Brackets are attached to teeth and connected with arch-wires that guide tooth movement.

5
Regular Adjustments

Periodic visits ensure steady progress and adjustments for optimal results.

6
Retention Phase

After braces are removed, retainers maintain the new alignment.

Types of Braces for Crowded Teeth

Metal Braces
  • Most common and effective
  • Suitable for all levels of crowding
  • Cost-effective option
Ceramic Braces
  • Tooth-colored brackets for a less noticeable look
  • Popular among adults
Self-Ligating Braces
  • Advanced system with fewer adjustments
  • Often more comfortable
Lingual Braces
  • Placed behind the teeth (invisible from front)
  • Ideal for aesthetic-conscious patients

Treatment Duration

  • Depends on the severity of case but typically ranges from 12 to 24 months
  • Complex cases may take longer
  • Consistent follow-ups are key for timely results
