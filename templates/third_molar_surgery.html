PROFESSIONAL WISDOM TOOTH REMOVAL

Advanced Extraction for Optimal Oral Health

Third molars, commonly known as wisdom teeth, are the last teeth to develop, typically emerging between ages 17-25. Due to limited space in modern jaws, these teeth often become impacted or cause various dental problems.

Our surgical expert Dr. Sandeep Prakash specializes in painless wisdom tooth extraction using minimally invasive techniques that preserve surrounding bone and tissue, ensuring faster healing and minimal post-operative discomfort.

WHEN NEEDED

When is Third Molar Surgery Needed?

Impacted Teeth

Teeth that don't have enough room to emerge properly or grow at abnormal angles, trapped in jawbone or gums.

Infection Risk

Partially erupted teeth prone to pericoronitis, decay, and bacterial infections due to difficult cleaning access.

Crowding Issues

Wisdom teeth pushing adjacent teeth out of alignment, causing orthodontic problems and bite issues.

Cyst Formation

If impacted 3rd molars are not removed surgically there are chances of cyst or tumor formation around the 3rd molar.

RECOVERY

Recovery Process

Day 1-3

Initial healing phase with some swelling and discomfort. Soft food diet and rest recommended.

Day 4-7

Significant improvement in symptoms. Gradual return to normal activities and diet progression.

Week 2-3

Most discomfort resolved. Sutures dissolve. Return to normal oral hygiene and most activities.

Week 4-6

Complete healing of soft tissues. Bone remodeling continues internally for several months.

POST-OP CARE

Post-Operative Care Instructions

Diet Guidelines

  • Soft food diet for 3-5 days
  • Avoid hot foods and liquids
  • No straws or sucking actions
  • Stay hydrated with water
  • Gradually reintroduce solid foods

Oral Hygiene

  • Gentle salt water rinses
  • Avoid brushing extraction site
  • Careful cleaning of other teeth
  • Use prescribed mouthwash
  • Resume normal hygiene after 1 week

Swelling & Pain Management

  • Cold compress for first 48 hours
  • Take prescribed medications
  • Keep head elevated when resting
  • Avoid strenuous activities
  • Follow medication schedule

What is Piezosurgery?

Piezosurgery is an advanced surgical technique that uses ultrasonic micro-vibrations to cut bone with extreme precision. Unlike traditional rotary instruments, it selectively cuts mineralized tissue while preserving soft tissues, making it ideal for complex extractions like impacted third molars.

Reduced Pain & Swelling

Clinical studies show patients often experience significantly lower postoperative pain and swelling compared to traditional methods.

Faster Recovery

Because the bone cuts are cleaner and more precise, the body typically reacts with less inflammation, leading to a quicker return to normal activities.

Nerve Protection

The technology is "tissue-selective," meaning it acts on mineralized bone while sparing delicate nerves (like the inferior alveolar nerve), blood vessels, and gums.

Greater Comfort

Piezosurgery produces less vibration and noise than traditional drills, which helps reduce patient anxiety and psychological stress.
